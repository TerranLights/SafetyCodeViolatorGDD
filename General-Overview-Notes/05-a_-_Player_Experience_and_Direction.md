# 04_Player_Experience_and_Direction

## Camera Style (Locked)
**2.5D Angled View** (~35–40° tilt) with free pan + smooth zoom. This gives a strong "evil god" feeling while making physics, falls, and chain reactions visually satisfying.

---

## Overall Player Experience (Expanded)

### Core Fantasy & Emotional Journey
You are a **highly competent, completely unhinged corporate saboteur** pretending to be a safety consultant. Every session should make the player feel:

- **Clever** during setup (planning evil genius moves)
- **Tense** during simulation (will my plan work? is the meter rising too fast?)
- **Giddy / Triumphant** when big chain reactions go off
- **Smug** during debrief when they get away with it

The game is ultimately about **creative mischief with consequences** — the joy comes from orchestrating beautiful disasters while barely staying under the radar.

### Typical Session Flow (15–30 minutes)

#### 1. Briefing Phase (1–2 minutes)
- Short, humorous corporate email or video call from your shady client.
- You get the location, budget, and twisted objectives.
- Tone: Dry corporate speak hiding malicious intent.
- Player feels: “Oh this is going to be fun…”

#### 2. Setup / Sabotage Phase (5–8 minutes)
- Calm, creative sandbox.
- Drag hazards into the world, adjust parameters, plan combos.
- You have time to think, experiment, and optimize.
- Feels like being an evil interior designer / engineer.
- Player feels: Focused, mischievous, powerful.

#### 3. Simulation / Chaos Phase (8–15 minutes)
- Hit “Start Shift” and workers arrive.
- Real-time simulation with full speed control.
- You can still intervene (drop emergency hazards, “fix” things to lower suspicion, distract workers).
- Big moments automatically trigger slow-motion + camera focus.
- Player feels: Anticipation → escalating excitement → chaotic joy → occasional panic when suspicion spikes.

#### 4. Debrief & Highlight Reel (2–3 minutes)
- Automatic compilation of the best (or funniest) moments.
- Score breakdown with lots of personality (“Kevin died for the 4th time this week…”).
- Unlock notifications and smug corporate “well done” message.
- Player feels: Satisfied, laughing, already thinking about improvements for the next shift.

### Moment-to-Moment Feel

- **Quiet moments** are short and purposeful (you can fast-forward).
- **Peak chaos** feels cinematic and rewarding.
- The game respects the player’s time — no long boring stretches.
- Failure is funny, not punishing (even a failed contract gives good laughs and partial unlocks).

### Progression of Player Mastery
- **Early game**: Learning to cause basic funny accidents.
- **Mid game**: Mastering subtle, high-plausibility combos and targeting specific workers.
- **Late game**: Orchestrating multi-stage masterpieces across large, complex locations while dancing on the edge of getting caught.

### Target Emotional Loop (per session)
**Anticipation → Creation → Chaos → Catharsis → Reflection**

This loop should feel **highly replayable** — players will come back for “just one more shift” to try a new combo or beat their high score.
