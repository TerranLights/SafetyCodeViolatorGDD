# Plausible Deniability
This system is the heart of the gameplay tension — pure chaos is easy, but **smart, sneaky chaos** is where the fun lives.

## Overview
The **Plausible Deniability Meter** is one of the core gameplay systems in *Safety Code Violator*. It is the main tension and skill mechanic — you must cause maximum chaos while making everything look like legitimate corporate incompetence.

## Meter Basics

- **Range**: 0% – 100%
- **Starting value**: 15–25% (varies by contract difficulty)
- **Fail State**: Reaching 100% → Investigation starts → You get fired / level failed

### How the Meter Changes

**Increases (Raises Suspicion):**
- Obvious single hazards (e.g. putting spikes on the floor)
- Multiple identical failures in a short time
- Workers with high "Suspicion" trait noticing patterns
- Using the same method too many times
- Major accidents happening too early in the shift

**Decreases (Lowers Suspicion):**
- Spreading out hazards over time
- Mixing many different types of violations
- Creating "logical" chains (e.g. wet floor → "forgotten" wet floor sign → someone slipping)
- Having workers "fix" things temporarily before the real accident
- Using corporate paperwork and fake safety meetings as cover

## Suspicion Sources & Values

| Action Type                    | Suspicion Impact | Notes |
|--------------------------------|------------------|-------|
| Single obvious trap            | High             | Very risky early |
| Chain reaction of small issues | Low–Medium       | Best approach |
| "Maintenance error"            | Very Low         | Excellent cover |
| Worker personality trigger     | Varies           | Paranoid workers raise it faster |
| Viral video potential          | Medium           | Public attention = higher scrutiny |

## Strategic Layers

1. **Short-term**: Managing the meter during the current shift
2. **Long-term**: Building reputation across multiple contracts (higher reputation = more powerful tools but also higher starting suspicion)

## UI Representation
- Visible meter in the top-right corner
- Color coded: Green → Yellow → Orange → Red
- Warning pop-ups when approaching critical levels
- "Close Call" highlights when you barely stay under the limit

## Advanced Mechanics (Unlocked Later)
- **Alibi System**: Spend budget on fake training sessions, safety audits, or scapegoat employees
- **Investigator Personalities**: Different investigators have different weaknesses
- **Reputation Decay**: If you stay too safe for too many contracts, suspicion starts higher next time (you're "too good" at your fake job)
