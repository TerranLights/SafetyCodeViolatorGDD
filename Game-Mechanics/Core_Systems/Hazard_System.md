# Hazard_System
This system is designed to be extremely fun to experiment with and highly replayable.

## Overview
The Hazard System is the creative core of *Safety Code Violator*. Players combine hundreds of individual hazards into complex, interlocking deathtraps.

## Hazard Categories

### 1. Environmental Hazards
- Wet floors, loose cables, unstable shelving, poor lighting, blocked exits
- Structural weaknesses (leaning ladders, cracked beams, overloaded elevators)

### 2. Equipment Sabotage
- Faulty machinery, overloaded electrical systems, malfunctioning safety devices
- "Repaired" tools that fail at the worst moment
- Mislabelled chemicals or incorrect PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)

### 3. Workflow & Policy Changes
- Dangerous new procedures ("to increase productivity")
- Removed safety protocols
- Encouraging risky behavior through fake incentives

### 4. Psychological / Human Factors
- Sleep-deprivation schedules
- Distraction campaigns (broken coffee machine, loud music, fake deadlines)
- Placing highly incompetent or aggressive coworkers in key positions

### 5. Advanced / Unlocked Hazards
- Smart sabotage (timer-based or trigger-based)
- Chain-reaction enablers
- "Acts of God" cover stories (fake weather, pest problems, etc.)

## Hazard Properties

Every hazard has these attributes:
- **Cost** (budget points)
- **Suspicion Value** (how much it raises the Plausible Deniability meter)
- **Lethality** (base chance of injury/fatality)
- **Trigger Type** (immediate, timed, worker-interaction, chain)
- **Cover Story Strength** (how believable it looks as an accident)
- **Combo Potential** (bonus when combined with other hazards)

## Combo System
The real depth comes from **hazard interactions**:
- Wet floor + unsecured ladder + heavy box on top = devastating combo
- Faulty coffee machine + sleep deprivation schedule + slippery tiles = comedy gold
- Overloaded power strip + flammable decorations + "no smoking" signs = ironic masterpiece

## Hazard Placement Rules
- Most hazards can be placed during Setup Phase
- Some can be dropped mid-simulation as "emergency interventions"
- Limited by budget and physical space in each location

## Progression
- Start with basic, low-suspicion hazards
- Unlock more creative, powerful, and subtle tools as you complete contracts
- Special "Signature Traps" that become your calling card (with achievements)
