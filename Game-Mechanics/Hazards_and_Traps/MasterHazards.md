# Master Hazards Document

**Safety Code Violator GDD**
*Universal trap/hazard reference. Hazards are available across multiple workplace types unless noted as environment-specific.*

---

## Design Philosophy

Hazards are presented with complete deadpan seriousness regardless of absurdity. The end-of-level safety report flatly states "worker injured by kangaroo" and moves on. The humor comes entirely from the gap between the clinical presentation and the chaos on screen.

Hazards are tiered by how suspicious they appear to the Safety Inspector:
- **Low Suspicion** — looks like a workplace accident. Contributes less to the PD meter per incident.
- **Medium Suspicion** — questionable but deniable. Moderate PD contribution.
- **High Suspicion** — obviously intentional. High PD contribution per incident. Use sparingly or cleverly.

## Chain Reaction Notes

The highest scoring plays involve low-suspicion traps triggering each other in sequence, keeping PD meter growth minimal while maximizing chaos. Example chains:

- Scorpions in boots → worker panics → runs into forklift → forklift hits barrel → barrel hits crane load → crane load drops onto foundation pit fencing → workers fall into crocodile moat
- Fog machine → worker can't see → walks into quicksand → Hero archetype rushes to help → also walks into quicksand → geese arrive for reasons of their own

## Animals as Active Hazards

Unlike placed traps, animals move semi-randomly once triggered — similar to the Safety Inspector. This means:
- Animals can trigger OTHER traps by wandering into them
- Animals can interfere with the inspector
- Animals cannot be fully controlled once released
- This is a feature, not a bug
