# Worker_AI_and_Personalities
This system turns passive watching into active entertainment and gives personality to the carnage.

## Overview
Workers are not just generic NPCs — they are the stars of the chaos. Each has a unique personality that affects how they interact with hazards, how much suspicion they generate, and how funny their deaths feel.

## Core Worker Attributes

- **Name & Job Role** (e.g. Kevin the Intern, Karen from HR, Chad the Sales Bro)
- **Personality Traits** (3–5 per worker)
- **Suspicion Level** (how likely they are to notice your sabotage)
- **Health & Resilience**
- **Behavior Patterns** (movement, routines, interactions)

## Personality Trait Examples

| Trait                  | Effect on Gameplay                                      | Humor Potential |
|------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Paranoid**           | Raises suspicion meter faster when seeing hazards       | High            |
| **Clumsy**             | Much more likely to trigger physical hazards            | Very High       |
| **Overachiever**       | Works faster → triggers timed traps earlier            | Medium          |
| **Gossip**             | Spreads awareness of hazards to other workers           | High            |
| **Coffee Addict**      | Frequently visits sabotaged coffee machine              | Excellent       |
| **Safety Officer**     | Actively looks for violations and lowers your meter if they "fix" things | High (ironic)   |
| **Distracted**         | Ignores obvious dangers while texting / daydreaming    | Very High       |

## AI Behavior

- Workers follow **daily routines** (arrive, coffee break, meetings, lunch, etc.)
- They react dynamically to the environment:
  - Avoid (or walk straight into) hazards based on traits
  - Help or warn other workers
  - Attempt to "fix" things you've sabotaged
- Chain reactions feel alive because workers influence each other

## Highlight System

- The game automatically detects and records the funniest moments involving specific workers
- Example: “Kevin tried to fix the coffee machine for the 4th time…”
- Special voice lines or text pop-ups for memorable deaths

## Progression & Variety

- Early levels: Mostly generic workers with 1–2 traits
- Later levels: Highly distinct characters with backstories and rivalries
- Special “Problem Employees” that are worth bonus points if eliminated creatively

## Design Goal
Every worker death should feel **personal and funny**, not generic. The player should start recognizing and targeting specific people across multiple shifts.
