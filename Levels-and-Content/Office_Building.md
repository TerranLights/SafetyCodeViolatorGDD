# Office Building — Level Design Document

**Safety Code Violator GDD**
*Second environment introduced in demo walkthrough. Available from start in full version.*

---

## Overview

The classic open-plan office — a medium-sized single-floor workplace with multiple interconnected areas, a high density of distracted workers, and excellent chain reaction potential. Strong corporate satire opportunities throughout.

This environment is naturally more forgiving than the Construction Site in terms of plausible deniability — office accidents are subtler, more deniable, and often funnier precisely because of how mundane they appear. A worker dying because of a faulty ergonomic chair quarterly KPI report is peak corporate satire.

**Tone:** Deadpan corporate satire. Ironic deaths. "Death by quarterly KPI report" energy. The safety report uses language like "workstation-related incident" and "beverage equipment malfunction." Everything is presented as a productivity and insurance matter.

**Chain reaction potential:** Medium-high. High worker density in open-plan areas creates excellent clustering opportunities. Subtler than the Construction Site but more satisfying for clever, low-suspicion chains.

---

## Crew

Variable crew size per shift:
- **Small crew:** 5–8 workers (skeleton staff, after-hours, weekend)
- **Standard crew:** 10–15 workers (normal business day)
- **Large crew:** 20+ workers (all-hands day, company event)

**Typical archetype mix for this environment:**
- Heavy Paranoid One and Gossip presence — office culture breeds both
- Strong Phone Addict representation — endemic to the environment
- At least one Health and Safety Rep per standard crew — ironic given the circumstances
- Supervisor archetype present on all but skeleton crews
- New Hire and Overachiever common in larger crews
- The Slacker is practically a mascot of the open-plan office

**Office-flavored worker color (not named characters — archetype inspiration):**
- The intern — maps to Clumsy + New Hire. Eager, lost, everywhere they shouldn't be
- The middle manager — maps to Supervisor + Paranoid. Delegates everything, notices suspicious things
- The HR rep — maps to Health and Safety Rep + Gossip. Warns others, spreads information fast
- The sales guy — maps to Overachiever + Phone Addict. Fast-moving, distracted, covers maximum ground

## Zones

### 1. Main Entrance / Reception
*Where workers arrive, sign in, and where the Safety Inspector spawns.*

**Worker Activities:**
- Arriving and departing
- Signing in at reception desk
- Waiting for meetings
- Delivery personnel dropping off packages

**Existing Environmental Hazards:**
- Revolving door at main entrance
- Wet lobby floor after rain
- Reception desk with ancient wiring
- Delivery packages stacked near entrance

**Trap Slots:**

| Trap | Suspicion Tier | Description |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Wet floor, no sign | Low | Classic. Someone mopped. Nobody was told |
| Tangled cables across walkway | Low | Trip hazard at industrial scale |
| Overloaded reception power strip | Low | Fire when enough devices running |
| Revolving door speed increased | Medium | Workers cannot exit or enter cleanly |
| Delivery stack improperly balanced | Medium | Tips onto whoever picks up the top package |
| Faulty automatic sliding doors | Medium | Opens and closes at wrong time |
| Revolving door at maximum speed | High | Deposits workers somewhere they didn't intend to go |
| Biometric scanner stores charge | High | Delivers on fingerprint contact. Escalates per use |
| Electronic visitor badges charged | High | Shock delivered when tapped to check in |

**Strategic Note:** Inspector spawns here. Same logic as Construction Site entrance — trapping this zone neutralizes the inspector early at the cost of high suspicion. Revolving door at maximum speed is particularly effective because it catches multiple workers in sequence.

### 2. Open-Plan Workspace
*The largest zone. High worker density, constant movement, excellent chain reaction potential.*

**Worker Activities:**
- Working at desks (loosely defined)
- Walking between desks for "collaboration"
- Attending impromptu standing meetings
- Avoiding actual work near the printer

**Existing Environmental Hazards:**
- Overloaded power strips at every desk cluster
- Rolling chairs on hard floors
- Precariously stacked monitors and equipment
- Filing cabinets with heavy top drawers
- Tangled cables everywhere

**Trap Slots:**

| Trap | Suspicion Tier | Description |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Faulty rolling chair | Low | Collapses or launches occupant |
| Overloaded power strip | Low | Electrical fire when threshold reached |
| Unstable filing cabinet | Low | Bottom drawer opened, top-heavy, tips |
| Whiteboard falls from wall | Low | Improperly mounted. Heavy frame |
| Wet floor patch between desks | Low | High-traffic area |
| Heavy printer falls from shelf | Medium | IT equipment improperly stored at height |
| All office chairs one-directional wheels | Medium | Only move toward windows. Gradual |
| Static electricity carpet plus exposed wiring | Medium | Workers become conductors |
| Standing desks locked at maximum height | Medium | Workers cannot reach anything, eventually fall |
| Faulty fluorescent lighting cascade | Medium | Entire floor's lights explode in sequence |
| Motivational poster avalanche | Medium | Entire hallway of heavy framed posters falls in sequence |
| All USB ports deliver full building voltage | High | Discovered one port at a time |
| Suspended ceiling grid rigged to drop | High | Tiles, lighting rigs, ventilation ducts, all at once |
| Printer network sends all jobs to one printer | High | Machine overloads, explodes with paper |
| Office supplies ordered in industrial quantities | High | Pallet of staplers on third floor. Floor gives way |

**Strategic Note:** This is the clustering zone. The Gossip and Crowd Follower archetypes ensure workers congregate here — a single well-placed chain reaction can catch multiple workers before anyone has time to flee. The Phone Addict is particularly reliable here — they will walk into anything.

---

### 3. Break Room / Kitchen
*High density of tired, distracted workers. The coffee machine is sacred and therefore the most reliable trap trigger in the building.*

**Worker Activities:**
- Making coffee (constant, ritualistic)
- Microwaving suspicious lunches
- Congregating near the water cooler
- Taking breaks that run slightly too long

**Existing Environmental Hazards:**
- Ancient coffee maker running constantly
- Microwave of unknown age
- Water cooler with questionable plumbing connection
- Refrigerator that nobody has cleaned since the company was founded

**Trap Slots:**

| Trap | Suspicion Tier | Description |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Coffee maker tampered | Low | Electrocutes or explodes on use. Inevitable |
| Commercial dishwasher flood | Low | Floor becomes extremely slippery |
| Refrigerator under pressure | Low | Contents unknown. Opens badly |
| Water cooler connected to hot supply | Medium | Scalding. Immediate |
| Microwave at wrong setting | Medium | Whatever is inside becomes a problem |
| Gas leak near microwave ignition | Medium | Microwave spark triggers explosion |
| Break room plumbing rerouted | Medium | Cold tap is boiling. Hot tap is ice cold |
| All hand dryers at industrial heat | Medium | Bathroom adjacent. Workers detour here |
| Coffee machine connected to wrong supply | High | Dispenses something industrial |
| Office holiday punch bowl on wrong tap | High | Connected to something that isn't punch |
| Walk-in refrigerator CO2 backup vents inward | High | If this office has one. Temperature drops instantly |

**Strategic Note:** The break room is a guaranteed gathering point — every worker visits it at least once per shift. The Lunch Guy archetype makes a beeline here regardless of any other conditions. The coffee machine is the single most reliable trigger in the entire game — every archetype except the Paranoid One will use it without hesitation.

---

### 4. Meeting Rooms
*Enclosed spaces with captive audiences. Workers trapped in meetings cannot easily flee.*

**Worker Activities:**
- Sitting through presentations they didn't need to attend
- Staring at projector screens
- Writing on whiteboards
- Disagreeing about the agenda

**Existing Environmental Hazards:**
- Projector mounted improperly overhead
- Whiteboard on wheels that rolls unexpectedly
- Uncomfortable chairs with questionable structural integrity
- Conference call systems with feedback potential
- Soundproofing that means nobody outside hears anything

**Trap Slots:**

| Trap | Suspicion Tier | Description |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Rigged conference chair | Low | Collapses when sat in |
| Projector falls from ceiling mount | Low | Improperly secured. Heavy |
| Whiteboard marker replaced with solvent | Low | Fumes build in enclosed room |
| Conference call feedback loop | Medium | Specific frequency. Shatters glassware |
| Allergen concentrate in room ventilation | Medium | Before big presentation. Workers incapacitated |
| Boardroom table on hidden hydraulics | High | Launches upward during meeting |
| Annual performance review triggers trapdoor | High | Results-based activation |
| Motivational speaker microphone feedback | High | Shatters glass in immediate area |
| Projector screen conceals mechanism | High | Something behind it |

**Strategic Note:** Meeting rooms are high-value enclosed traps. Workers inside cannot easily flee, and the Freezer archetype is especially dangerous here — locking up in a confined space with an active hazard. The soundproofing note is important: incidents in meeting rooms generate less immediate panic in adjacent zones, giving chains more time to develop before the inspector responds.

---

### 5. Supply Closet / Server Room
*High lethality potential but higher suspicion risk. Workers don't normally go here without reason — which makes it a trap for the curious.*

**Worker Activities:**
- Fetching supplies (briefly)
- IT staff maintaining servers (longer visits)
- The Investigator archetype specifically gravitates here
- People trying to find somewhere quiet

**Existing Environmental Hazards:**
- Electrical panels with questionable maintenance history
- Overloaded server racks generating significant heat
- Unstable shelving stacked to ceiling
- Poor ventilation in server room

**Trap Slots:**

| Trap | Suspicion Tier | Description |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Unstable supply shelving | Low | Falls when wrong item removed |
| Overloaded server room heat buildup | Low | Gradual. Sprinklers activate eventually |
| Ancient HVAC pumps something unpleasant | Low | Into ventilation. Gradual effect |
| EMP device disguised as server rack | High | Knocks out all electronic safety systems simultaneously |
| Server room UPS battery bank short-circuit | High | Enormous heat and charge simultaneously |
| Velociraptor in supply closet | High | Universal. Every workplace has a storage room |
| Something in the server room fridge | Low | Nobody labeled it. Nobody should open it |
| Ventilation fan blades loosened | High | Detach at speed, travel through ductwork |

**Strategic Note:** This zone rewards patience. Workers only visit here briefly — the Investigator archetype is the most reliable target. The EMP is the highest-value trap in the building: knocking out all electronic safety systems simultaneously means elevators freeze, security doors slam, lights go out, and the inspector loses access to half the building.

---

### 6. Elevators / Stairwell
*Transit zone. Workers pass through constantly but rarely linger. High throughput, excellent placement for sequential traps.*

**Worker Activities:**
- Moving between floors (if multi-floor variant)
- Using stairs when elevator is "out of order"
- The Runner archetype inevitably ends up here

**Existing Environmental Hazards:**
- Elevator with maintenance history
- Stair railing of uncertain structural integrity
- Fire door at top of stairwell

**Trap Slots:**

| Trap | Suspicion Tier | Description |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Loose stair railing | Low | Standard building negligence |
| Elevator doors open on wrong floor | Medium | Worker steps out into nothing |
| Stairwell handrails electrified | High | Discovered on contact |
| Elevator cable frayed | High | Drops one floor at a time before going all the way |
| Fire escape stairs replaced with decorative ones | High | Hold no weight |
| Escalator at triple speed wrong direction | High | If building has one. Deposits workers badly |

**Strategic Note:** The stairwell is a funnel — workers who flee other zones often end up here. Placing a trap at the stairwell exit after triggering chaos in the open-plan workspace creates reliable sequential catches.

## The Safety Inspector

Spawns at Main Entrance/Reception when PD meter maxes out. Full behavior in Manager_Behavior.md.

**Office specific notes:**
- Cannot access server room easily — door access may require keycard
- Meeting room soundproofing slows inspector response to incidents inside
- EMP trap in server room can disable inspector's ability to identify active traps temporarily

---

## The Manager (Department Head / Middle Manager)

Stays near their office or the meeting rooms. Conducts walkthroughs of open-plan workspace periodically. Full behavior in Manager_Behavior.md.

**Office specific notes:**
- Retreats to private office when danger detected — private office can be trapped
- Directs workers into meeting rooms during walkthroughs — concentrates workers in enclosed space
- Worth significant bonus points — hardest worker in the building to catch due to frequent retreating behavior
- Backup group when Stage 2 triggers: HR department plus senior colleagues

**Stage 3 authorities:** HR Department + Employment Lawyers (immediate) followed by relevant regulatory body depending on incident nature

---

## Plausible Deniability Notes

Office environments have naturally lower baseline PD generation than industrial sites — accidents here look more plausible as genuine workplace mishaps. However:
- Multiple incidents in the same zone within a short time window raises suspicion quickly
- The Health and Safety Rep archetype actively reports suspicious things to the Manager, potentially accelerating escalation
- EMP and structural collapse traps generate very high PD regardless of environment

## Level Variants Particularly Suited to This Environment

From Level_Variants.md — these variants have especially strong synergy with the office setting:

- **After-Hours / Night Shift** — skeleton crew, different worker routines, security guard as primary target, cleaning staff present
- **All-Hands Day** — maximum crew, entire company present, Manager is easier to find but harder to catch
- **Inspection Day** — HR has scheduled a formal review, inspector on-site from simulation start
- **Budget Cuts** — half the office furniture already structurally compromised before setup
- **Haunted Shift** — former employees who died of "overwork" have returned. The safety report will simply note "worker startled by apparition near filing cabinet"
