# Art_Style_Guide
This guide keeps the game visually appealing while making the violence feel comedic rather than disturbing.

## Overall Visual Direction
**Clean, colorful, cartoonish corporate aesthetic** that contrasts sharply with the dark comedy.

Think: *Untitled Goose Game* + *The Sims* + bright corporate training videos.

## Art Style Pillars

- **Stylized / Cartoonish** (not realistic)
- Bright, saturated colors during normal operation
- Increasing visual chaos and particle effects as disasters unfold
- Strong silhouettes and readable shapes (important for mobile)
- Exaggerated animations for deaths and reactions

## Key Visual Elements

### Characters (Workers)
- Big heads, expressive faces (simple but readable emotions)
- Distinct silhouettes and clothing colors by role (intern = hoodie, manager = tie, etc.)
- Exaggerated ragdoll physics on death (flailing limbs, spinning, etc.)

### Environment
- Clean, modern corporate/industrial look at start of shift
- Gradual destruction: papers flying, cracks, smoke, sparks, puddles
- Strong contrast between "before" (pristine) and "after" (disaster zone)

### Hazards & Objects
- Highly readable icons and outlines
- Cartoonish details (e.g. coffee machine with evil-looking steam)
- Clear "broken" states with sparks, leaks, wobbling

### UI Style
- Corporate PowerPoint / HR training aesthetic
- Clean sans-serif fonts
- Blues, greens, and grays with red/orange accents for danger
- Subtle scanline or "digital display" effects on meters

## Camera & Presentation
- Top-down / slightly angled 2.5D isometric view
- Smooth zoom and pan
- Dramatic slow-motion camera during highlight moments
- Dynamic camera shakes and particle focus on big combos

## Color Palette
- **Normal State**: Bright, professional (blues, teals, soft oranges)
- **Escalating Chaos**: Increasing reds, yellows, smoke, fire particles
- **Highlight Reels**: High contrast + bloom effects

## Technical Notes
- 2D sprites with some normal mapping for depth
- Particle systems for smoke, sparks, liquid, debris
- Godot-friendly: optimized for mobile performance
- Scalable assets for different screen sizes
